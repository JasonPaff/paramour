**Breaking:** the `/client` subpath is retired in favor of per-router entries (design-06 Group 4). The app hooks move unchanged to `@paramour-js/next/app` (`"use client"` retained) and are now gated to `AnyAppRoute`, so a pages-branded route at an app hook call site is a compile error. New `@paramour-js/next/pages` entry — deliberately without a `"use client"` directive — exports `useRouteParams` / `useSearch` over `next/router` returning the three-state `RouterResult<T>` (`SafeResult<T> | { status: "pending" }`): `pending` until `router.isReady` (only ever observed on statically-optimized pages; `getServerSideProps` pages are ready from the first render), then the familiar `success` / `error` arms — a decode failure never throws. `useSearch` subtracts the route's path-param names from the merged `router.query` before decoding, mirroring `parseContext`'s server-side split. Calling the pages hooks from a component mounted under the App Router rethrows `next/router`'s unmounted error as a `ParamourError` naming the `/app`-vs-`/pages` mistake. There are deliberately no `OrThrow` variants on `/pages`: throwing on `pending` would flash error boundaries on static-optimized first renders — pair the hooks with `route.parseContext` in `getServerSideProps` instead. Each entry's bundle graph stays disjoint (`next/navigation` unreachable from `/pages` and vice versa), asserted on the built dist.
